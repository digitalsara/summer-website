Senate Emblem

Adopted in 2008, the Senate Emblem is a heraldic emblem developed by the Chief Herald of Canada. It combines the shield of the Arms of Canada with an image of the Senate Mace upright.

The shield of the Arms of Canada shows the royal symbols of the United Kingdom and France, along with a sprig of maple leaves below, images that represent Canadians of all origins. The Senate Mace is a symbol of royal authority, parliamentary privilege and the authority of the Senate and its Speaker. It rests on the Clerk’s table during sittings of the Senate.

  1. Senate Mace
  2. The three royal lions of England
  3. The Irish harp of Tara
  4. The lion of Scotland
  5. The fleur-de-lis of France
  6. Sprig of three maple leaves

Printing colours and file transfers

Senate Printing Services can print your documents in black and white or colour. Please refer to the colour chart for the recipes of the official Senate red.

We accept files in the following formats: PDF, InDesign, Publisher, Excel and Word.
